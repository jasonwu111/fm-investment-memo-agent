FM MEMO RULES BUILDER — CALIBRATION CASE NOTES v2

PURPOSE

This file is the calibration-case manifest and boundary guide for the FM Memo Rules Builder. It identifies how approved historical memos and source packages may be used to test and improve the reusable Schema and Drafting Rules.

It is not an authoritative source for fund facts. It does not replace the underlying final memos, Source Packages, or current control documents.

CALIBRATION-ONLY RESTRICTION

Historical final memos and Source Packages are for authorized methodology calibration and validation only. They must not be treated as factual knowledge for a future fund and should not remain attached to a production memo-writing agent.

Transfer into production only the human-approved reusable Schema, Drafting Rules, production instructions, and the one current target-fund source boundary selected for that run.

CASE TYPES

1. Paired calibration case:
   - Includes an approved final memo and corresponding Source Package.
   - May be used for structure, tone, source retrieval, evidence mapping, and transformation analysis.

2. Memo-only reference case:
   - Includes an approved final memo but no confirmed corresponding Source Package in the calibration environment.
   - May be used for structure, presentation, section placement, and tone.
   - Must not be used to infer source hierarchy or claim that a final statement came from a particular source type.

3. Held-out validation case:
   - Must be explicitly designated by the user.
   - Its final memo remains unavailable to the simulated drafting step until the evidence map or draft is complete.

No case is held out automatically.

PAIRING RULE FOR PAIRED CASES

The Approved Final Memo and Source Package within the same attached calibration folder form a proposed pair. Verify the pairing through fund identity, content, dates, and folder context before reverse mapping. If evidence contradicts the proposed pairing, hold the affected analysis and request clarification.

TARGET IDENTITY REQUIREMENT

Before extracting or reverse mapping a paired case, establish:

- Firm.
- Target opportunity.
- Strategy and vintage.
- Commercial fund name.
- Legal investment vehicle, when available.
- Related and predecessor funds.
- Source Package boundary.

Do not assume that the recommendation title, economic opportunity, legal fund name, GP entity, and FM investment vehicle are identical.

SOURCE-PACKAGE TRIAGE GUIDANCE

First scan folder names, filenames, dates, versions, and target-fund references. Use these as routing clues and verify content before using facts.

Initial priorities, adapted to actual naming:

1. Legal folders and clean/current term summaries or term sheets.
2. Clean/current/final/executed LPA and governing documents.
3. Team info, team structure, organization charts, rosters, biographies, and Key Person materials.
4. DDQ, due-diligence files, meeting materials, and FM notes.
5. PPM, fundraising decks, portfolio construction, strategy, track-record, and value-creation materials.
6. Other files needed to close a gap or resolve a conflict.

Deprioritize files labeled redline, right line, blackline, comparison, compare, marked, markup, track changes, changes, or diff when a clean/current/final/executed version is available. Use comparison files when analyzing changes, when no clean version exists, or when resolving a material conflict; record the limitation.

Filename matches are not evidence. Confirm the target fund, vehicle, class, date, version, and relevant content passage.

REVERSE-MAPPING RULE

For a paired case, the approved memo may identify the expected output or a topic to investigate. The underlying Source Package must support the reverse-mapped evidence. If support is not located after a reasonable coverage sweep, label the statement `not traced in the reviewed materials` and describe the search scope.

Do not infer that every approved-memo statement must be present in the currently connected package; historical writers may have had access to other records.

GENERALIZATION RULE

For every observation, classify it as:

- Reusable common practice.
- Strategy- or structure-specific variant.
- Flexible presentation choice.
- Case-specific fact.
- Potential methodology gap.
- Uncertain due to incomplete evidence.

Only the first three categories may ordinarily influence reusable methodology, and strategy-specific variants must retain an appropriate inclusion trigger.

SENSITIVE-INFORMATION HANDLING

- Keep calibration materials within authorized case and account boundaries.
- Do not use public web or unrelated external sources unless the user explicitly authorizes them.
- Do not publish, externally share, or place raw case materials into production knowledge.
- Reusable methodology outputs should avoid unnecessary reproduction of case-specific people, terms, figures, or conclusions.
